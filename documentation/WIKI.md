## Chess Terms {#chess-terms-en}

### Basic Concepts

**Opening** - initial stage of game (first 10-15 moves)
- Goal: develop pieces, control center, castle

**Middlegame** - middle of the game
- Goal: attack, tactics, positional play

**Endgame** - final stage
- Goal: realize advantage, technique

### Tactical Motifs

**Fork** - one piece attacks two or more opponent pieces
```
Example: knight on e5 attacks queen on d7 and rook on g6
```

**Pin** - piece cannot move as it would expose more valuable piece
```
Example: bishop on b5 pins knight on c6 to king on e8
```

**Double Attack** - simultaneous attack on two targets
```
Example: pawn takes on d5 and attacks pieces on c6 and e6
```

**Discovered Check** - check revealed by moving piece
```
Example: bishop on c4, knight on e5, king on e8 - knight moves, bishop gives check
```

**Back Rank Mate** - rook or queen gives mate on 1st or 8th rank
```
Example: rook on e8, king on g8 trapped by own pawns
```


### Positional Concepts

**Center** - squares e4, d4, e5, d5
- Center control gives mobility advantage

**Open File** - vertical without pawns
- Ideal for rooks

**Weak Square** - square that cannot be defended by pawns
- Good place for knight or bishop

**Isolated Pawn** - pawn without neighboring pawns on adjacent files
- Weakness, but gives activity

**Passed Pawn** - pawn not blocked by opponent pawns
- Strong in endgame

**Pawn Structure** - pawn arrangement
- Determines game plan

## Learning {#learning-en}

### For Beginners

#### Lesson 1: Basics

**Game objective:**
- Checkmate opponent's king
- Checkmate = king in check and cannot escape

**Piece values:**
- Pawn = 1
- Knight = 3
- Bishop = 3
- Rook = 5
- Queen = 9
- King = priceless

**Opening principles:**
1. Control center (e4, d4, e5, d5)
2. Develop pieces (knights and bishops)
3. Castle early (protect king)
4. Don't move same piece twice
5. Don't bring queen out too early

#### Lesson 2: Basic Checkmates

**Queen and King mate:**
1. Push king to edge of board
2. Use your king to help
3. Deliver mate on last rank

**Two Rooks mate:**
1. Rooks on adjacent ranks
2. Alternately push king back
3. Mate on last rank

**Rook and King mate:**
1. Push king to corner
2. Use king to cut off
3. Deliver mate with rook

### For Intermediate

#### Lesson 3: Tactics

**How to find tactics:**
1. Check all checks
2. Check all captures
3. Check all threats

**Typical motifs:**
- Undefended pieces
- Overloaded pieces
- Weak back rank
- Open lines to king

**Practice:**
- Solve puzzles in üß© Puzzles section
- Start with "Mate in 1"
- Gradually increase difficulty


#### Lesson 4: Strategy

**Position evaluation:**
1. Material (who has more pieces)
2. King position (safety)
3. Pawn structure
4. Piece activity
5. Space control

**Planning:**
- Find weaknesses in opponent position
- Improve your piece positions
- Create threats
- Prevent opponent plans

### For Advanced

#### Lesson 5: Endgame

**Key positions:**

**King and Pawn vs King:**
- Square rule
- Opposition
- Key squares

**Rook Endgame:**
- Lucena position
- Philidor position
- Rook behind passed pawn

**Bishop Endgame:**
- Opposite colored bishops = draw
- Same colored bishops = technique

#### Lesson 6: Opening Preparation

**Popular openings for White:**
- 1.e4 - Open games (Italian, Spanish)
- 1.d4 - Closed games (Queen's Gambit)
- 1.Nf3 - Reti Opening
- 1.c4 - English Opening

**Popular openings for Black:**
- Against 1.e4: Sicilian, French, Caro-Kann
- Against 1.d4: Slav, Nimzo-Indian, Grunfeld

**Study principles:**
- Understand ideas, don't memorize moves
- Study typical plans
- Analyze your games
